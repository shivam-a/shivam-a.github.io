# Intro
Professional Transport Planner and a wannabe Data Scientist – I assign vehicles to a road network and hope they match reality. Founder of Absolutely Nothing™, but a proud overthinker of everything from junction flares to why my Python scripts still error out. I use data science to automate tasks so I can spend more time explaining to my family and friends what a "transport modeller" actually does. Proficient in making Excel cry, writing code that almost runs, and blaming “calibration issues” for all life’s problems. 

# Some History

- At ages 5-17, Professional “medium achiever” – aced the art of not burning out. Played basketball (poorly), survived 12 years of all-boys school (zero game), and chose Science and then Civil Engineering (again for no game) over literally anything else. Spoiler: Should’ve sold dad’s automobile parts.
- At age 18, Discovered girls exist in College that I can talk to. Panicked. 
- At age 19, Became a professional event volunteer (read: free labor) for college fests. Joined ASCE-VIT Chapter's Technical Team, Eklavya to build bridges (metaphorical ones, mostly) and concrete canoes. 
- At age 20, “Marketing Head” for the steel bridge team = made promotional videos and begged strangers for money. Won a fancy award in the U.S. (Doctors Bayers Award ≈ “We Tried” trophy).
- At age 21, Rejected adulthood, applied to two grad schools. Picked Germany over Canada because tuition-free wasn't there, thand (coldness) was lesser and doorie (distance from India) was samller.
- At age 22, Graduated as a “Civil” Engineer (emphasis on civil – still figuring that out). Moved to Munich, learned winters here are basically loneliness. Bonus: First breakup and rebound in the same year. Efficiency!
- At age 23, Culture shock + homelessness (thanks, German housing crisis!). Survived on Pakistani and Punjabi hospitality, odd jobs (flipping burgers at Five Guys 🍔), and sheer delusion.
- At age 24, Mastered the art of crying when codes didn't work during an internship that I bagged at EiFER. Mom’s health nosedived. Realized adulthood is just a series of “uh-oh” moments.
- At age 25, COVID hit. Family got COVID, Germany got lockdowns, I got three jobs (assistantship, internship, delivery job at Liefrando). Thesis? Done in 4 caffeine-fueled nights. (Note: Do not recommend.)
- At age 26, in 2022. Life said: “Plot twist!” Mom’s health crisis + thesis deadlines + job offers = multitasking champion 🏆. Defended my thesis from India (Wi-Fi > dignity). Same year, Moved from Kolkata to Hyderabad, swapped 3 jobs for ✨unemployment✨. Mom got a new lung (shoutout to science and Waheguruji).  God, in a chaotic neutral mood, sent a LinkedIn message from Prof. Amit Agarwal (MATSim wizard). Landed a remote gig fixing Java code. Conclusion: Divine intervention runs on Wi-Fi.
- At age 27, Promoted from “ad-hoc” to “full-time researcher” (translation: still underpaid). Realized academia’s currency is “exposure,” not rent money. Applied to every transport firm alive. Systra hired me because someone finally ignored my lack of “UK project experience” (Jacobs/WSP: 🙃). Now I automate SATURN models while questioning life choices.
- At age 28, Met Shivaani on Hinge in April. Lost Mom in October. Props to my manager for letting me cry-work from home, and to KIMS Hospital for keeping Mom comfy. 
- At age 29, Current vibe: Surviving Systra, building this website (midlife crisis?), and pretending adulthood isn’t a glitch.
